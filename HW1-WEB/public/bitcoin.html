Bitcoin (BTC), created in 2008 by the pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto, is the world’s first successful cryptocurrency, overcoming decades of failed digital cash experiments. Unlike traditional monetary systems controlled by trusted third parties, Bitcoin’s monetary policy is maintained through a combination of software, cryptography, and financial incentives.

At the heart of Bitcoin is the blockchain, a cryptographically secure and verifiable database that powers the network. The Bitcoin ecosystem is supported by a global network of stakeholders, including miners who secure the network and manage the issuance of Bitcoin, traders who speculate on its value, and developers who work to integrate more people into the cryptocurrency space.

At Cointelegraph, we document the evolving narrative of Bitcoin and the emergence of a borderless, permissionless financial system. We explore the changes since Satoshi Nakamoto's white paper, the progression of Bitcoin trading, the best investment strategies, and the impact of Bitcoin futures on blockchain adoption. We also examine how industry stakeholders are striving to make Bitcoin a staple in everyday life and the response of established financial systems to this transformative shift.
